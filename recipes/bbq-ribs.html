Simple BBQ Ribs

Description

This recipe is for simple and tasty BBQ ribs. First, prepare the ribs by removing the membrane and seasoning them with a homemade dry rub. Grill the ribs over indirect heat for about 2 to 2.5 hours, occasionally basting with BBQ sauce. During the last 15-30 minutes, apply more BBQ sauce to create a delicious glaze. Serve and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs (about 2-3 pounds each)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Olive oil or vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Prepare the ribs:
    • Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs. You can use a butter knife to loosen the membrane, then grip it with a paper towel and peel it off.
  2. Create the dry rub:
    • In a small bowl, mix the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper to create the dry rub.
  3. Season the ribs:
    • Rub a light layer of olive oil or vegetable oil on both sides of the ribs to help the dry rub adhere better.
    • Generously sprinkle the dry rub over the ribs, making sure to coat both sides evenly. Pat the rub into the meat for better adherence.
  4. Preheat the grill:
    • Preheat your grill to medium heat. If using a charcoal grill, wait until the coals are covered with ash and spread them out in an even layer.
  5. Grill the ribs:
    • Place the ribs on the grill over indirect heat. If using a gas grill, turn off the burner(s) directly below the ribs, or if using a charcoal grill, position the ribs away from the coals.
    • Close the grill lid and let the ribs cook for about 2 to 2.5 hours. During this time, you may occasionally baste the ribs with BBQ sauce for added flavor.
  6. Finish with BBQ sauce:
    • During the last 15-30 minutes of cooking, brush a generous layer of BBQ sauce on both sides of the ribs. Allow the sauce to caramelize and create a sticky glaze.
  7. Rest and serve:
    • Once the ribs are fully cooked and the sauce has caramelized, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
    • Cut the ribs between the bones and serve with additional BBQ sauce on the side.

Enjoy your delicious and simple BBQ ribs! They pair well with coleslaw, cornbread, or grilled vegetables.
